# Zhihu Discussion: The Role of 'Scenarios' in Product Management

## Summary
A detailed discussion thread on Zhihu where experienced product managers debate and explain the fundamental importance of "scenario thinking" (场景思维) in product design. The conversation moves beyond superficial definitions to explore how deep scenario analysis improves requirement accuracy, feature comprehensiveness, market positioning, and team alignment.

## Key Themes
- **Beyond Environment**: Multiple contributors distinguish between a shallow understanding of "environment" (where/when) and a deeper analysis of the complete "user story" within a scenario.
- **Practical Frameworks**: Contributors provide systematic methods for conducting scenario analysis, illustrated with concrete examples from consumer electronics, fitness apps, e-commerce, and on-demand services.
- **Methodological Integration**: The discussion connects scenario analysis to established [[User-Centered Design (UCD)]] methodologies, showing how it complements tools like [[Persona (用户画像)]] and [[Use Case (用例)]].
- **Contrast with Feature-Focused Design**: A recurring theme is the shift from function-oriented design to problem-solving design that addresses authentic user challenges.

## Notable Perspectives
- **[[David 周子轩]]**: Emphasizes the critical distinction between "environment" and "user story," using examples of a smart water cup and a fitness app to demonstrate how this distinction leads to better product decisions.
- **[[刘飞]]**: Provides a structured, systematic framework for scenario analysis, breaking it down into components like user identity, motivation, and emotional needs, with examples from smartphone camera features.
- **[[郑坚义]]**: Frames the discussion within the broader [[User-Centered Design (UCD)]] methodology, explicitly linking [[Scenario Analysis (场景分析)]] to Persona and Use Case tools.

## External References
Contributors reference influential thinkers to support their arguments:
- **[[Marty Cagan]]**: Cited from *Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love* for emphasizing understanding user stories over feature lists.
- **[[Jeff Bezos]]**: Referenced for the principle of focusing on enduring customer needs ("what won't change in ten years") as a way to identify stable scenarios.
