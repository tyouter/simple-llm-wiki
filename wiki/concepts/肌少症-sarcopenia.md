# 肌少症 (Sarcopenia)

## Definition
Age-related loss of muscle mass, strength, and function, identified as a primary cause of physical decline in middle age. This condition typically begins around age 40 and accelerates without intervention.

## Impact on Middle-Aged Adults

As discussed in [[Zhihu]], sarcopenia is a central concern for the 40-50+ demographic. It contributes to:
- Reduced metabolic rate
- Increased fall risk
- Decreased mobility and independence
- Higher susceptibility to chronic diseases

## Countermeasures

Contrary to common belief, cardio exercise alone is insufficient to combat sarcopenia. Effective approaches include:

1. **Strength Training**: Resistance exercises targeting major muscle groups
2. **Protein Intake**: Adequate dietary protein to support muscle maintenance
3. **Progressive Overload**: Applying the [[超负荷原则 (Overload Principle)]] to stimulate adaptation
4. **Compound Movements**: Exercises like squats, deadlifts, and presses that engage multiple muscle groups

## Prevention vs. Reversal

While complete reversal may be challenging, significant prevention and mitigation are possible through consistent strength training. As noted in the Zhihu discussion, addressing sarcopenia should be a primary goal of middle-aged exercise programs.
