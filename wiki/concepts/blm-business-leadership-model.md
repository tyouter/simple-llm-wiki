# BLM (Business Leadership Model)

## Definition
A comprehensive strategic planning and execution methodology originally developed by [[IBM]] and widely adopted by companies like [[华为 (Huawei)]]. The model provides a structured framework for connecting strategy formulation with execution.

## Model Structure

### Strategy Formulation (左侧)

#### 1. Strategic Intent
- Vision, mission, and strategic goals
- Aspirational targets and market positioning
- Long-term direction and purpose

#### 2. Market Insight
- Understanding market trends and disruptions
- Customer needs and pain points
- Competitive dynamics and industry evolution
- External environment analysis

#### 3. Innovation Focus
- Identifying areas for business model innovation
- Product/service innovation opportunities
- Operational innovation possibilities
- Strategic partnerships and ecosystems

#### 4. Business Design
- Customer value proposition
- Profit model and revenue streams
- Strategic control points
- Scope of operations and organization

### Strategy Execution (右侧)

#### 5. Key Tasks
- Critical initiatives and projects
- Milestones and timelines
- Resource requirements
- Risk management plans

#### 6. Formal Organization
- Organizational structure and design
- Decision rights and governance
- Performance management systems
- Processes and workflows

#### 7. Talent
- Key positions and capabilities
- Skills development and training
- Leadership pipeline
- Culture and values alignment

#### 8. Culture
- Organizational values and behaviors
- Change management approach
- Employee engagement strategies
- Innovation culture development

## Key Principles
- **Integrated Approach**: Connects strategy with execution seamlessly
- **Market-Driven**: Emphasizes external market insights
- **Leadership-Centric**: Focuses on leadership alignment and commitment
- **Iterative Process**: Allows for continuous refinement and adjustment

## Adoption by Chinese Enterprises
- [[华为 (Huawei)]] has successfully implemented BLM for strategic management
- Adapted to Chinese business context and culture
- Used to drive growth and competitive advantage in technology sectors

## Comparison with Other Frameworks
- More comprehensive than traditional strategic planning approaches
- Stronger emphasis on execution and organizational alignment
- Integrates leadership development with strategy implementation
