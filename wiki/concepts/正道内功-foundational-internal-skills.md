# 正道内功 (Foundational Internal Skills)

## Definition
A metaphor describing basic movement patterns (breathing, balance, hip hinge) as the essential "internal martial arts" that prevent injury and enable effective exercise. These skills form the foundation upon which all other physical training is built.

## The Three Foundational Skills

As described by [[谷磨]] in [[Zhihu]]:

1. **Breathing/Core Integration**
   - Proper diaphragmatic breathing
   - Core activation and stabilization
   - Ribcage and pelvic alignment

2. **Foot/Balance Foundation**
   - Foot arch activation and stability
   - Ankle mobility and control
   - Single-leg balance capacity
   - Ground force transmission

3. **Hip Hinge Pattern**
   - Proper bending mechanics
   - Glute and hamstring activation
   - Spinal neutrality maintenance
   - Load distribution through hips

## Philosophical Approach

The "internal skills" concept emphasizes:
- **Quality Over Quantity**: Mastering movement before adding load or volume
- **Prevention Focus**: Building resilience against [[代偿 (Compensation)]] and injury
- **Lifetime Foundation**: Skills that support all daily activities and sports
- **Mind-Body Connection**: Conscious movement practice rather than automatic execution

## Practical Application

For middle-aged adults beginning exercise:
1. **Assessment**: Identify limitations in the three foundational areas
2. **Isolated Practice**: Train each skill separately with focus and feedback
3. **Integration**: Combine skills in simple movements like squatting or lifting
4. **Progression**: Only add external load or complexity when foundations are solid

## Relationship to Other Concepts

- **Prerequisite to Strength Training**: Enables safe application of the [[超负荷原则 (Overload Principle)]]
- **Prevention of Sarcopenia**: Supports the strength training needed to combat [[肌少症 (Sarcopenia)]]
- **Alternative Perspective**: Contrasts with immediately starting specific activities like swimming or weight training
