**Iterative Skill Development** is the core practice of treating Skill creation as a cyclical process of testing, evaluation, feedback, and refinement, rather than a one-time writing task. Inspired by the [[Skill-Creator Tool]] workflow, it emphasizes that skills are *engineered*, not just written. The process involves establishing [[Baseline A/B Testing|baselines]], defining quantifiable success metrics ([[Skill Evaluation & Assertions|assertions]]), gathering feedback (automated and human), and making targeted improvements. This mindset justifies the upfront investment because the output is a reusable [[Skill as Asset|asset]]. Improvement heuristics include generalizing from feedback, keeping skills lean ([[Modular & Focused Skill Design]]), explaining the 'why' behind instructions, and codifying patterns into scripts. It represents the application of a product development lifecycle to AI capability creation.
