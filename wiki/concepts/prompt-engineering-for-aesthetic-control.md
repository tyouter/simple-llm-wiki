The practice of crafting, curating, and combining input prompts specifically to exert fine-grained control over the aesthetic and stylistic output of generative AI models. This moves beyond basic functional prompt engineering into the realm of artistic direction, treating prompts as modular design components that can be assembled and processed by the AI to achieve a specific, high-fidelity visual goal.

In the context of frontend development, this involves using pre-built XML style prompts from repositories like [[designprompts.dev]] to guide tools like [[Claude Code]] away from its default visual tendencies. The advanced technique involves downloading multiple prompt files and instructing the AI to combine them or synthesize a new, customized design specification (e.g., `MyAppDesign.xml`) based on them. This method is the **primary-method** for achieving [[AI-Free Interface Aesthetics]] and serves as a critical **quality-lever** on the [[AI-Generated Design Quality Spectrum]]. It is a **core-activity** within an [[AI-Native Design Workflow]], shifting the human role from manual implementer to director and curator of aesthetic constraints.
