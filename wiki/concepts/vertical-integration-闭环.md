# Vertical Integration/闭环

## Definition
A business strategy where a company controls multiple stages of its production and supply chain, typically moving upstream into supplying its own inputs or downstream into distribution. In Chinese industrial discourse, it is often referred to as forming a "closed loop" (闭环), emphasizing self-sufficiency and control. This is a cornerstone of [[BYD (比亚迪)]]'s strategy and a key component of building [[Industrial Moats/护城河]].

## BYD's Implementation
As analyzed in [[Zhihu Discussion: Financial vs. Industrial Logic in BYD's Development Strategy]], BYD's vertical integration is exceptionally deep:
1. **Battery Production:** Originally a battery maker, BYD produces its own lithium-ion cells (including the blade battery), packs, and battery management systems.
2. **Semiconductors:** Designs and manufactures IGBT chips and other automotive semiconductors through its subsidiary BYD Semiconductor.
3. **Other Core Components:** Produces electric motors, electronic control units, and even automotive glass.
4. **Final Assembly:** Controls the entire vehicle assembly process.

## Advantages
1. **Cost Control:** Eliminates supplier markups and captures profit margins across the chain.
2. **Supply Chain Resilience:** Reduces dependency on external suppliers, mitigating risks like chip shortages.
3. **Technology Protection & Synergy:** Keeps proprietary technology in-house and allows for tighter integration between components (e.g., battery-to-chassis design).
4. **Speed of Innovation:** Changes in one component can be quickly coordinated with changes in others.

## Disadvantages & Criticisms
1. **High Capital Intensity:** Requires massive upfront investment in diverse manufacturing capabilities.
2. **Risk of Insularity:** May miss out on best-in-class innovations from specialized external suppliers.
3. **Management Complexity:** Running multiple, distinct businesses within one company is challenging.

The strategy aligns strongly with **Industrial Logic** as defined in [[Financial vs. Industrial Logic]], favoring long-term control and incremental improvement over the flexibility and potentially higher margins of a focused, asset-light model favored by financial markets.
