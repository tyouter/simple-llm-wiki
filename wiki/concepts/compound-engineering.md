# Compound Engineering

## Definition
[[Compound Engineering]] (复利工程) is a development methodology that emphasizes knowledge accumulation and reuse. Each engineering task generates 'compound interest' by documenting plans, execution details, reviews, and lessons learned for future reuse. This creates a growing knowledge base that accelerates future development cycles.

## Core Philosophy
The methodology is based on the principle that engineering work should not just solve immediate problems but also create reusable assets that make future work easier and more efficient. Each task contributes to a knowledge repository that grows in value over time.

## Key Components

### Knowledge Documentation
Every engineering task includes comprehensive documentation of:
- Planning and design decisions
- Execution details and implementation approaches
- Review findings and quality assessments
- Lessons learned and improvement opportunities

### Reuse Mechanisms
Systems for storing, organizing, and retrieving accumulated knowledge:
- Structured knowledge bases
- Search and retrieval systems
- Context-aware recommendation engines
- Versioning and evolution tracking

### Process Integration
Compound engineering is integrated into standard development workflows:
1. **Plan**: Document objectives and approaches
2. **Execute**: Record implementation details
3. **Review**: Capture evaluation insights
4. **Compound**: Structure knowledge for future reuse

## Contrast with Other Approaches

### vs. Vibe Coding
Contrasts sharply with [[Vibe Coding]], which focuses on short-term productivity gains using high-level aesthetic prompts. Compound engineering prioritizes long-term knowledge accumulation over immediate speed.

### vs. Traditional Documentation
Goes beyond traditional documentation by making knowledge reuse a first-class concern in the engineering process, not an afterthought.

## Relationship with Harness Engineering

[[Compound Engineering]] complements [[Harness Engineering]] by providing the knowledge management framework that enables AI agents to learn from past work. While harness engineering focuses on AI-driven development processes, compound engineering ensures the knowledge generated during those processes is preserved and leveraged.

## Implementation Examples

### Every's Methodology
The organization [[Every]] developed the 'Compound Engineering' methodology and has implemented it in their development practices.

### Practical Applications
In the context of [[Harness Engineering and Compound Engineering for AI-Driven Development]], compound engineering provides the knowledge accumulation layer that makes AI-driven development sustainable and continuously improving.

## Benefits

1. **Accelerated Development**: Reusable knowledge reduces time spent on similar tasks
2. **Quality Improvement**: Lessons learned prevent repetition of past mistakes
3. **Team Scaling**: Knowledge preservation helps onboard new team members
4. **AI Agent Enhancement**: Provides training data and context for AI systems

## Challenges

1. **Initial Overhead**: Requires discipline to document comprehensively
2. **Knowledge Organization**: Creating effective retrieval systems can be complex
3. **Maintenance**: Keeping accumulated knowledge current and relevant

## Industry Context

Compound engineering represents a shift toward more systematic knowledge management in software development, particularly relevant in the age of AI-assisted development where knowledge can be leveraged by both humans and AI agents.

## Future Directions

As AI systems become more integrated into development workflows, compound engineering is likely to evolve toward:
- Automated knowledge extraction from development activities
- AI-assisted knowledge organization and retrieval
- Predictive reuse suggestions based on current context
- Integration with [[Agent-First Development]] systems
