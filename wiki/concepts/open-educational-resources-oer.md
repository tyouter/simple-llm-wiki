# Open Educational Resources (OER)

## Definition
Teaching, learning, and research materials that reside in the public domain or have been released under an open license that permits no-cost access, use, adaptation, and redistribution by others. This concept was a major practical outcome of the [[Zhihu Discussion: Comparative Analysis of Foreign vs. Domestic Textbooks]], where users actively shared links to such resources.

## Types of OER Highlighted in the Discussion
1. **Open Textbooks**: Complete, peer-reviewed textbooks available for free online or at low cost in print.
   - *Example*: Open Textbook Library.
2. **Open Courseware (OCW)**: Free and open digital publication of university-level course materials.
   - *Example*: Course materials from Harvard, Stanford, Carnegie Mellon.
3. **Interactive Learning Platforms**: Free online platforms offering structured lessons, practice, and feedback.
   - *Example*: [[Khan Academy (可汗学院)]], especially praised for humanities subjects.
4. **Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs)**: Free online courses available to large numbers of people.
   - *Related Entity*: Pioneers like [[吴恩达]] have been instrumental in this area.

## Connection to Textbook Discussion
The discussion frames OER as a powerful solution to several critiques:
- **Accessibility**: Mitigates the high cost of quality textbooks, particularly foreign imports.
- **Self-Study Support**: Many OER platforms are inherently designed for independent learning, aligning with the ideal of [[Self-Study Friendly Textbook Design]].
- **Pedagogical Diversity**: Provides alternatives to the [[Pedagogical Approach Comparison|pedagogical approaches]] found in locally available textbooks.

## Significance and Trends
OER represents a global movement toward democratizing education. It supports lifelong learning, continuous skill development (as seen in discussions like [[Zhihu Discussion: Career Transition to Strategic Management and Consulting]]), and the work of self-directed learners and educators on platforms like Zhihu.
