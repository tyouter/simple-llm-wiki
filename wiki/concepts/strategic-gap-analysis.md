# Strategic Gap Analysis

## Definition
The process of identifying and quantifying the differences between an organization's current state and its desired strategic objectives. This analysis helps organizations understand what needs to change to achieve their strategic goals.

## Types of Gaps

### 1. Performance Gaps
- Differences between current performance levels and target performance
- Measurable gaps in financial metrics, market share, or operational efficiency
- Often addressed through improvement initiatives and optimization

### 2. Opportunity Gaps
- Untapped market potential or new business opportunities
- Emerging trends or technologies not currently leveraged
- Strategic moves competitors haven't made
- Requires innovation and new capabilities

## Analysis Process

### Step 1: Define Current State
- Assess current performance metrics
- Evaluate existing capabilities and resources
- Analyze current market position
- Review organizational strengths and weaknesses

### Step 2: Define Desired State
- Clarify strategic objectives and targets
- Identify future market opportunities
- Define required capabilities and resources
- Establish performance benchmarks

### Step 3: Identify Gaps
- Quantitative analysis of performance differences
- Qualitative assessment of capability gaps
- Market opportunity analysis
- Competitive positioning gaps

### Step 4: Prioritize Gaps
- Impact assessment (strategic importance)
- Feasibility analysis (resources and timeline)
- Risk evaluation (implementation challenges)
- Dependency mapping (prerequisite gaps)

### Step 5: Develop Closure Strategies
- Performance improvement plans
- Capability development initiatives
- Market entry or expansion strategies
- Organizational change programs

## Integration with Planning Frameworks
- Core component of the [[7-Step Strategic Planning Framework]] (Step 3)
- Implicit in [[BLM (Business Leadership Model)]] market insight phase
- Connects strategy formulation with execution requirements

## Implementation Challenges
- **Data Limitations**: Incomplete or inaccurate performance data
- **Bias**: Over-optimism in target setting or current state assessment
- **Organizational Resistance**: Reluctance to acknowledge gaps
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited ability to address identified gaps
