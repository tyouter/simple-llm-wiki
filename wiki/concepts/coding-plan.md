# Coding Plan

## Definition
Fixed monthly subscription plans offered by Chinese AI companies for developers, replacing traditional pay-per-token pricing with capped usage limits for AI programming tools. These plans emerged in 2026 as a response to the unpredictable costs associated with [[AI Tool Chaining/Combination]] and automated workflows.

## Purpose and Benefits

The primary purpose of coding plans is [[Token Burning Prevention]] - preventing unexpected high costs from AI agents running overnight or in loops. This is particularly important for frameworks like [[OpenClaw]] and other automated development tools.

### Key Benefits:
1. **Predictable Costs**: Fixed monthly fees replace variable token-based billing
2. **Usage Caps**: Clear limits prevent budget overruns
3. **Multi-Model Access**: Many plans provide access to multiple AI models through a single subscription
4. **Developer-Focused**: Optimized for programming tasks rather than general AI use

## Market Context
Coding plans represent a significant shift in the Chinese AI market, with major providers including [[Alibaba Cloud Bailian]], [[ByteDance Volcano Engine]], [[Zhipu AI]], and [[MiniMax]] offering competing plans. These plans often position themselves as alternatives to [[Claude Code]], with some claiming 3-5x the usage of Claude Pro.

## Technical Features
Modern coding plans typically include:
- Native support for [[Model Context Protocol (MCP)]]
- Integration with local development environments
- High-speed inference options (up to 100 TPS in some cases)
- Various refresh mechanisms (monthly, 5-hour windows, sliding windows)

## Disruption by NVIDIA NIM
The emergence of [[Free Model Integration via NIM]] through [[NVIDIA NIM (NVIDIA Inference Microservices)]] presents a potential challenge to the coding plan subscription model, offering free access to premium models like [[GLM-5]] and [[Kimi K2.5]].
