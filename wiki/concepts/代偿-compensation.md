# 代偿 (Compensation)

## Definition
A movement dysfunction where improper movement patterns or muscular weaknesses cause other muscles, joints, or structures to work abnormally to complete a task. Over time, this leads to chronic pain, injury, and further movement deterioration.

## Relevance to Middle-Aged Exercise

In the context of [[Zhihu]], compensation is identified as a critical concern because:

1. **Age-Related Changes**: Reduced flexibility, strength imbalances, and previous injuries increase compensation risk
2. **Foundation First**: As emphasized by [[谷磨]], addressing compensation requires mastering [[正道内功 (Foundational Internal Skills)]] before engaging in specific training
3. **Injury Prevention**: Proper movement patterns prevent the cumulative damage caused by chronic compensation

## Common Compensation Patterns

- **Hip Hinge Compensation**: Using the lower back instead of glutes and hamstrings
- **Shoulder Compensation**: Overusing neck and upper traps during arm movements
- **Knee Compensation**: Altered gait patterns due to weak hips or ankles
- **Breathing Compensation**: Using accessory muscles instead of diaphragm

## Assessment and Correction

1. **Movement Screening**: Identifying asymmetries and limitations
2. **Mobility Work**: Addressing joint restrictions
3. **Stability Training**: Strengthening stabilizer muscles
4. **Motor Pattern Re-education**: Relearning proper movement mechanics
