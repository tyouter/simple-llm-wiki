# Technical Blog Quality Hierarchy

## Definition
A proposed ranking system for technical content sources, suggesting that personal independent blogs maintained by practitioners offer the highest quality information, while corporate blogs and content farms rank lowest.

## Hierarchy Levels
1. **Personal Independent Blogs**: Written by individual practitioners, framework creators, or researchers. Considered highest quality due to direct expertise and lack of editorial constraints.
2. **Curated Social Media Platforms**: Such as high-quality Zhihu answers or specialized communities where content is peer-reviewed.
3. **Technical Publications**: Industry magazines, journalistic outlets, and established technical media.
4. **Corporate/Company Blogs**: Content produced by organizations, often with marketing or recruitment angles.
5. **Content Farms/Aggregators**: Low-quality, SEO-optimized sites with minimal original insight.

## Context
This concept emerged from a [[Zhihu]] where users expressed frustration with [[AI Content Saturation]] on mainstream social media platforms. The hierarchy reflects a growing preference for authentic, practitioner-written content over algorithmically promoted material.

## Driving Factors
- **Authenticity**: Independent blogs often contain unfiltered technical insights and lessons learned from real projects.
- **Depth**: Without word limits or editorial constraints, bloggers can explore topics in greater technical detail.
- **Timeliness**: Practitioners can share insights immediately rather than through delayed publication processes.
- **Specialization**: Many technical bloggers focus on narrow domains where they have deep expertise.

## Relation to Programmer Culture
The hierarchy connects to the [[Programmer-to-Blogger Transition]], where successful programmers often establish blogs that become authoritative sources in their technical domains.

## Practical Implications
- **Content Discovery**: Suggests prioritizing RSS subscriptions to individual blogs over social media feeds.
- **Credibility Assessment**: Provides a framework for evaluating technical content sources.
- **Career Development**: Highlights blogging as a valuable activity for establishing technical authority.
