# Prompt-Dependent Output Quality

## Definition
The observed characteristic of AI design tools where the quality and usefulness of the generated output are highly dependent on the user's skill in crafting effective prompts and understanding the tool's capabilities. Results can range from highly polished to unusable based primarily on input quality.

## Manifestation in Design Tools
In tools like [[Google Stitch]], the same underlying AI model ([[Gemini]]) can produce dramatically different results based on:
- **Prompt Specificity:** Vague vs. detailed descriptions
- **Aesthetic Language:** Use of design terminology and style references
- **Functional Requirements:** Clear specification of interactive behaviors
- **Context Provision:** Background information about target users and use cases

## Implications for Adoption
- **Learning Curve:** Users must develop [[Design Prompt Engineering for UI]] skills
- **Inconsistent Results:** Novice users may get poor results while experts get excellent ones
- **Barrier to Entry:** Contradicts the 'democratization' narrative of AI tools
- **Professional Advantage:** Designers with prompt engineering skills gain competitive edge

## Relationship to AI Aesthetics
Poor prompting often results in generic, stereotypical 'AI-looking' interfaces that lack originality and polish. This connects to concerns about [[AI-Free Interface Aesthetics]] and the risk of homogenized design output.

## Mitigation Strategies
1. **Prompt Libraries:** Curated collections of effective prompts (like [[designprompts.dev]])
2. **Iterative Refinement:** Using AI feedback to improve prompts
3. **Hybrid Workflows:** Combining AI generation with manual refinement
4. **Skill Development:** Training in effective prompt construction for design
