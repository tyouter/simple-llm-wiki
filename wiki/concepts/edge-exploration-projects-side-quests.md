# Edge Exploration Projects (Side Quests)

## Definition
Short-term autonomous experiments where team members spend limited time (typically an afternoon) building prototypes to test AI model capabilities or push models beyond their expected limits. These projects are used to discover new product features organically rather than through planned development.

## Purpose and Value

### Discovery Mechanism
Edge Exploration Projects serve as a discovery mechanism in [[AI-Exponential Product Management]] by:
- Testing the boundaries of current AI model capabilities
- Identifying unexpected use cases or features
- Providing empirical data on what's possible with current models
- Uncovering opportunities that wouldn't emerge from traditional planning

### Low-Risk Experimentation
Because these projects are:
- **Time-boxed**: Typically limited to a few hours
- **Autonomous**: Individuals or small groups can initiate them
- **Low-cost**: Using tools like [[Claude Code]] for rapid prototyping
- **Non-critical**: Not tied to core product roadmaps

They represent a low-risk way to explore potentially high-value opportunities.

## Implementation at Anthropic
According to [[Cat Wu]], these projects are part of Anthropic's shift toward rapid experimentation. They complement the broader [[Prototype-First Development]] approach by:
1. **Decentralizing innovation**: Allowing team members to explore independently
2. **Accelerating discovery**: Finding new capabilities faster than planned research
3. **Building intuition**: Developing team understanding of model capabilities through hands-on experience

## Workflow Integration
These projects typically use:
- **[[Claude Code]]** for building working prototypes quickly
- **Natural language interaction** rather than traditional coding
- **Rapid iteration** based on immediate results
- **Informal sharing** of discoveries with the team

## Relation to Model Improvements
As AI models improve exponentially (as measured by organizations like [[METR]]), these exploration projects become increasingly valuable because:
- New capabilities emerge with each model release
- Yesterday's impossible task might be today's afternoon project
- Continuous exploration helps teams stay ahead of capability curves

## Organizational Impact
[[Bihan Jiang]] notes that this approach increases the ceiling for high-quality product teams by shortening the path from idea to prototype.

Described by Cat Wu as part of Anthropic's product management transformation.
