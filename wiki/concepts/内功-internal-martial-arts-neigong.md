# 内功 (Internal Martial Arts / Neigong)

## Definition
内功 (*neigong*) refers to internal energy cultivation practices in Chinese martial arts, involving breath control, mental focus, and specific body postures. The term literally means "internal skill" or "internal work."

## Traditional Perspective
From traditional martial arts viewpoints, 内功 represents:
- **Energy cultivation**: Developing and circulating "气" (*qi*) or internal energy
- **Mind-body integration**: Coordinating breath, intention, and movement
- **Health foundation**: Building internal strength that supports external techniques
- **Progressive development**: Skills that develop through dedicated practice over time

## Modern Interpretations
In the [[Zhihu Discussion: Internal Martial Arts and Breathing Techniques]], contrasting views emerge:

### Physiological Interpretation
- **Core muscle engagement**: Sensations attributed to 内功 may result from exercising underutilized core muscle groups
- **Neuromuscular coordination**: Improved body awareness and control through specific training
- **Breath mechanics**: Enhanced respiratory efficiency through practiced techniques

### Traditional Practice Interpretation
- **Cultivation system**: A coherent method for developing specific internal capacities
- **Experiential reality**: Tangible sensations and effects reported by practitioners
- **Cultural transmission**: Knowledge passed through teacher-student lineages

## Key Debates
1. **Metaphysical vs. Physical**: Whether 内功 involves special "energy" or is explainable through known physiology
2. **Efficacy across demographics**: Whether benefits are more noticeable in certain populations (e.g., older vs. younger practitioners)
3. **Teachability**: Whether these skills can be systematically taught or require special transmission
