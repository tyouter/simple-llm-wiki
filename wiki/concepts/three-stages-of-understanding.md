# Three Stages of Understanding

## Definition
A framework describing the cognitive and psychological journey toward deeper comprehension and [[Enlightenment (开悟)]]. It is often metaphorically described using the phrase "看山是山，看山不是山，看山还是山" (Seeing a mountain as a mountain, then not as a mountain, then again as a mountain).

## The Three Stages

### Stage 1: Naive Realism / Acceptance of Defaults (看山是山)
- **Description**: The individual accepts reality and explanations at face value. They operate using the default cognitive models provided by society, culture, or authority.
- **Cognitive State**: Low prediction error because sensory input is forced to conform to the simple, default model.
- **Relation to [[Brain Prediction Model]]**: The brain predominantly uses the energy-efficient Path 1 (reinterpretation) to maintain the default model.

### Stage 2: Cognitive Dissonance / Deconstruction (看山不是山)
- **Description**: The individual encounters experiences or information that cannot be reconciled with their default models. This leads to doubt, confusion, and the realization that their previous understanding was incomplete or wrong.
- **Cognitive State**: High prediction error. The existing model is breaking down.
- **Challenge**: This stage is often uncomfortable and can lead to cynicism or nihilism if one gets stuck.

### Stage 3: Flexible / Constructive Understanding (看山还是山)
- **Description**: The individual develops the ability to hold multiple, context-dependent models of reality. They can see the "mountain" again, but with a deeper appreciation of its complexity and their own perceptual construction of it. This is the stage associated with enlightenment.
- **Cognitive State**: Managed prediction error. The individual has developed the skill to use [[Brain Prediction Model|Path 2 (model updating)]] when appropriate.
- **Outcome**: Wisdom, adaptability, and reduced existential anxiety.

## Practical Significance
- **For Personal Growth**: Provides a map for navigating the inevitable confusion that accompanies deep learning or life changes.
- **For Education**: Suggests that creating productive dissonance (Stage 2) is essential for moving students beyond rote learning (Stage 1).
- **For [[Personal Transformation through Incremental Action]]**: The journey through these stages requires the consistent discipline advocated in practical transformation advice.
