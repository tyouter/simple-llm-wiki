# Scenario Analysis (场景分析)

## Definition
A core product design and requirements analysis method that involves constructing a detailed, holistic understanding of the context in which a user interacts with a product. It goes beyond identifying the superficial physical environment (where and when) to model the complete "user story"—encompassing the user's identity, goals, motivations, specific challenges, emotional state, and the sequence of actions they take to achieve an outcome.

## Core Distinction: User Story vs. Environment
As emphasized in the [[Zhihu Discussion: The Role of 'Scenarios' in Product Management]], a key insight is differentiating:
- **Environment (场景)**: The superficial context (e.g., "in the office," "on a mobile phone," "while commuting").
- **User Story (用户故事)**: The narrative that includes *who* the user is, *why* they are there, *what* problem they're trying to solve, *how* they feel, and *what* a successful outcome looks like.
True scenario analysis focuses on the latter.

## Purpose and Benefits
1.  **Improves Requirement Accuracy**: By grounding features in real user narratives, it reduces the risk of building solutions for hypothetical or non-existent problems.
2.  **Enhances Feature Comprehensiveness**: Reveals edge cases and secondary needs that a pure functional specification might miss.
3.  **Clarifies Market Positioning**: Helps define what job the product is hired for, distinguishing it from competitors.
4.  **Aligns Cross-Functional Teams**: Provides a shared, concrete story that developers, designers, and marketers can reference, moving beyond abstract feature lists.
5.  **Reveals [[Mental Model (心智模型)]]**: Helps designers understand the user's internal model of how a system should work, leading to more intuitive interfaces.

## Methodological Context
As framed by [[郑坚义]], scenario analysis is one pillar of the [[User-Centered Design (UCD)]] toolkit, working in concert with:
- **[[Persona (用户画像)]]**: Defines who the user is.
- **Scenario (场景)**: Defines the context and story in which the persona acts.
- **[[Use Case (用例)]]**: Defines the specific interaction steps between the user and the system within that scenario.

## Practical Application
Contributor [[刘飞]] provides a framework for analysis, suggesting a scenario description should include:
- **User Identity & Role**: Who is involved?
- **Motivation & Goal**: What are they trying to achieve?
- **Context & Environment**: Where and when is this happening?
- **Process & Challenges**: What steps do they take? What obstacles do they face?
- **Emotional Needs**: How do they feel during this process? What emotional state are they seeking?
- **Product's Role**: How does the product help them overcome challenges and achieve their goal?

## Contrasting Philosophy
Scenario analysis represents a shift from **[[Function-Oriented vs. Problem-Solving Design|Function-Oriented Design]]** ("Let's add a reminder feature") to **Problem-Solving Design** ("How can we help a forgetful office worker stay hydrated throughout their busy day?").
