# Post-Forum Internet Landscape

## Definition
The shift away from large, centralized, and general-purpose online forums (like the iconic [[Tianya Forum (天涯论坛)]]) towards a more fragmented and specialized ecosystem of websites, platforms, and communities. This landscape is characterized by numerous [[Niche Knowledge & Creative Hubs]] catering to specific interests, rather than a few monolithic destinations hosting all discussions.

## Characteristics
- **Fragmentation & Specialization**: The web is populated by sites dedicated to narrow domains (e.g., ancient texts, UI design, programming tutorials), each serving as a high-quality alternative to the noise of general social media.
- **Distributed Communities**: Social interaction and knowledge exchange occur across many independent sites rather than within a single walled garden.
- **Discovery by Curation**: Finding valuable sites relies less on search engine algorithms and more on [[Curated Digital Exploration]]—recommendation lists, forum threads (like the source [[Zhihu Discussion: Alternative Websites After Tianya Forum Closure]]), and personal networks.

## Drivers
- The closure or decline of major forums (the "post-forum" condition).
- User desire for higher signal-to-noise ratio and deeper expertise.
- The ability for smaller, passionate groups to maintain dedicated online spaces.
