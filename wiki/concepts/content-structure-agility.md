**Content Structure Agility** measures how easily and rapidly users can reorganize and restructure the hierarchical and sequential relationships within their digital notes. High agility reduces the cognitive and mechanical friction of changing one's mind about information architecture, supporting non-linear thinking and iterative knowledge refinement.

Tools that implement the [[Text Block as a Unit of Manipulation]] paradigm directly enhance content structure agility by making blocks directly movable rather than requiring tedious copy-paste-indent operations. The [[Obsidian Dragger]] plugin explicitly targets this agility improvement, allowing users to experiment with narrative flow, argument structure, or information hierarchy through intuitive drag-and-drop.

This capability complements [[AI-Enhanced Note-Taking]] workflows where AI assistants might suggest structural reorganizations based on content analysis. When content structure agility is high, users are more likely to engage in continuous refinement of their notes, treating them as living documents rather than static artifacts. The psychological effect is significant: reduced friction lowers the barrier to improvement, encouraging ongoing optimization of knowledge organization.
