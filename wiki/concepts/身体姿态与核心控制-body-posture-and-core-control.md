# 身体姿态与核心控制 (Body Posture and Core Control)

## Overview
A system of body alignment and control centered on the "两横一纵" (two horizontals, one vertical) structure, described in the [[Zhihu Discussion: Internal Martial Arts and Breathing Techniques]]. The method aims to optimize body mechanics for health and movement efficiency.

## Structural Framework

### 两横一纵 (Two Horizontals, One Vertical)
1. **First Horizontal**: Shoulder line - keeping shoulders level and relaxed
2. **Second Horizontal**: Hip line - maintaining pelvic alignment
3. **Vertical**: Spine - maintaining natural curvature and alignment

### Key Alignment Principles
- **挺腰拔背** (*ting yao ba bei*): Straightening waist and back without stiffness
- **Open torso**: Creating space for organs and unrestricted breathing
- **Natural stacking**: Aligning joints in their optimal load-bearing positions
- **Dynamic stability**: Maintaining alignment during movement, not just static poses

## Core Control Components

### Diaphragmatic Integration
- **Breath-posture connection**: Using breath to support and refine posture
- **Internal pressure management**: Balancing intra-abdominal pressure for stability
- **Organ positioning**: Creating optimal space for organ function through alignment

### Muscle Coordination
- **Deep stabilizers**: Engaging deeper core muscles rather than superficial tension
- **Balanced engagement**: Avoiding over-recruitment of any single muscle group
- **Proprioceptive awareness**: Developing sensitivity to subtle alignment shifts

## Practical Applications

### Daily Life Integration
- **Sitting posture**: Applying principles to desk work and relaxation
- **Walking mechanics**: Efficient gait through proper alignment
- **Lifting technique**: Protecting spine during load bearing

### Martial Arts Context
- **Power generation**: Efficient force transmission through aligned structure
- **Balance maintenance**: Stability in dynamic situations
- **Injury prevention**: Reducing strain on joints and connective tissues

## Health Benefits
- **Reduced back pain**: Through optimal spinal loading
- **Improved breathing**: Unrestricted diaphragm movement
- **Better digestion**: Organ positioning that supports function
- **Enhanced circulation**: Reduced vascular compression

## Relationship to Other Concepts
- **Foundation for [[呼吸法 (Breathing Method)]]**: Proper posture enables optimal breathing
- **Physical basis for [[内功 (Internal Martial Arts / Neigong)]]**: Alignment as prerequisite for energy cultivation
- **Connection to [[丹田与核心肌群 (Dantian vs. Core Muscles)]]**: The anatomical reality behind traditional concepts

## Training Approach
- **Awareness first**: Developing sensitivity before attempting correction
- **Gradual refinement**: Small, sustainable adjustments over time
- **Contextual application**: Adapting principles to different activities
