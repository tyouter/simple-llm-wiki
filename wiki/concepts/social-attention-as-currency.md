# Social Attention as Currency

## Definition
An economic and social theory emerging from the analysis of the [[Zhihu Discussion: Personal Development and Life Philosophy Insights]]. It posits that in the digital, information-saturated age, collective human attention has become a fundamental form of capital. The ability to reliably capture and hold attention is a primary driver for acquiring other resources, including money, influence, and opportunities.

## Mechanism
1.  **Scarcity Shift:** While information is abundant, human attention is severely limited. This scarcity makes attention valuable.
2.  **Signal Function:** A [[Personal Work/Product (作品)]] that gains attention acts as a powerful signal. It demonstrates unique value, skill, or perspective, differentiating the creator from the anonymous crowd.
3.  **Value Assignment:** Society tends to assign economic value and social status to that which it pays attention to. Attention validates and amplifies.

## Role of the "Personal Work"
The personal "work" is the vehicle for converting individual creativity into attention capital. It is not the attention itself that is the end goal, but what the attention enables: trust, authority, network growth, and monetization opportunities.

## Contrast with Traditional Models
This concept updates traditional models of career success (climbing a corporate ladder based on private performance reviews). Success is increasingly public and market-based: you build an audience or a reputation around your public work, which then opens doors.

## Implication for Individuals
It incentivizes moving from private competence (being good at your job) to public creation (making things that are valuable and visible to a broader audience). It underpins the urgency behind creating a [[Personal Work/Product (作品)]].
