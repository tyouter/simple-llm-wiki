# Service Trade Globalization

## Definition
The next major phase of economic globalization, extending beyond the trade of physical goods to encompass the cross-border exchange of services. This trend is being unlocked by digital technologies, primarily **AI Agents**, which remove the traditional frictions that historically rendered many services "non-tradable."

## Historical Barrier: Why Services Were Local
Unlike goods, services often required:
1.  **Simultaneity**: Provider and consumer needed to interact in real-time.
2.  **Proximity**: Many services (legal, consulting, healthcare) relied on local knowledge, trust, and regulation.
3.  **Communication Friction**: Language and cultural understanding were significant barriers.
4.  **Coordination Costs**: Managing work across time zones was complex.

## The Enabling Technology: AI Agents
AI Agents act as friction-removers:
- **Language**: Real-time, high-quality translation.
- **Time Zones**: Asynchronous coordination and 24/7 operation.
- **Local Knowledge & Trust**: Can be encoded and delivered digitally; reputation becomes platform-based.
- **Process Coordination**: Automate handoffs, quality control, and client communication.

## Economic Impact
This shift exposes a vast swath of the global economy—professional, technical, and creative services—to international competition for the first time. It is the foundational trend that makes phenomena like the [[Super Individual (超级个体)]] and the predicted [[Agent Shock]] possible.

## Connection to Other Concepts
- **[[Agent Shock]]**: The disruptive consequence of service trade globalization accelerating rapidly.
- **[[Super Individual (超级个体)]]**: The type of professional who can leverage this globalized market most effectively.
