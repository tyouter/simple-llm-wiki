# Philip Kotler

## Description
A foundational marketing theorist and professor at Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management. Kotler is often called 'the father of modern marketing' for his systematic approach to marketing management and strategy.

## Key Contributions

### Marketing Principles
Kotler's textbook *Marketing Principles* (often published as *Marketing Management*) is considered the definitive work in marketing education. Key concepts include:
- The 4Ps of marketing (Product, Price, Place, Promotion)
- Market segmentation, targeting, and positioning
- Customer relationship management
- Marketing strategy and planning

## Role in the Discussion
In the Zhihu discussion about career transition to strategic management, Kotler's *Marketing Principles* is included in [[Sunny]]'s 'minimum required reading list.' This reflects the importance of marketing knowledge within the broader field of strategic management and the need for [[Cross-Disciplinary Knowledge for Management]].

## Importance for Strategic Consultants
- Marketing strategy is a core component of business strategy
- Understanding customer behavior and market dynamics is essential for strategic recommendations
- Kotler's frameworks provide tools for analyzing and addressing market challenges

## Position in Learning Frameworks
In a [[Strategic Management Learning Framework]], marketing knowledge would typically be part of the cross-disciplinary expansion phase, complementing core strategy theory with specific functional expertise.
