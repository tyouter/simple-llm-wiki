# David 周子轩

## Description
A Zhihu user and product manager who contributed a key perspective to the [[Zhihu Discussion: The Role of 'Scenarios' in Product Management]]. His answer is notable for clearly articulating and emphasizing the critical distinction between "environment" and "user story" within scenario analysis.

## Key Contribution
He argued that many product teams mistake a superficial description of the environment (where/when) for a true scenario analysis. He posited that a complete scenario must include the user's story: their identity, goals, motivations, and the specific challenges they face.

## Illustrative Examples
He used practical examples to demonstrate the impact of this distinction:
1.  **Smart Water Cup**:
    - *Environment-focused analysis*: "Used in the office." Might lead to features like a sleek design.
    - *User story analysis*: "A forgetful knowledge worker, trying to build a hydration habit amidst back-to-back meetings, often misses cues to drink water." This reveals needs for gentle, non-disruptive reminders, progress tracking against a daily goal, and perhaps integration with calendar apps to schedule drinking breaks.
2.  **Fitness App**:
    - *Environment-focused analysis*: "Used at the gym."
    - *User story analysis*: "A beginner feeling self-conscious at the gym, unsure about proper form, and needing guidance and encouragement to complete a workout." This points to needs for clear video demonstrations, form feedback, and a supportive, non-judgmental tone.

His examples concretely show how deepening the analysis from environment to user story directly leads to more accurate, comprehensive, and user-valued features.
