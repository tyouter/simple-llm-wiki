# BYD (比亚迪)

## Overview
BYD (Build Your Dreams) is a Chinese multinational conglomerate, primarily known as a leading manufacturer of electric vehicles (EVs), batteries, and related components. Founded in 1995 as a battery maker, it has grown into one of the world's largest EV producers. The company is central to the analysis in [[Zhihu Discussion: Financial vs. Industrial Logic in BYD's Development Strategy]], where it is portrayed as a paradigm of **Industrial Logic**.

## Key Strategic Pillars
1. **[[Vertical Integration/闭环]]:** BYD controls a remarkably deep supply chain, producing its own batteries (including the proprietary Blade Battery), semiconductors (IGBT chips via BYD Semiconductor), motors, and many other core components. This strategy builds resilience and cost advantages.
2. **Technological Innovation:** Key innovations include:
    - **Blade Battery:** A lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery with a novel cell-to-pack design, praised for safety and cost.
    - **DM-i & DM-p Hybrid Platforms:** Super hybrid technology that prioritizes fuel efficiency and performance.
    - **e-Platform 3.0:** A dedicated EV architecture.
3. **Scale & Cost Leadership:** Massive manufacturing scale allows BYD to achieve low production costs, a critical [[Industrial Moats/护城河]] in the competitive EV market.

## Leadership & Culture
Led by founder and CEO [[Wang Chuanfu (王传福)]], an engineer by training, the company culture is described as intensely focused on engineering, manufacturing, and incremental improvement. This contrasts with a more finance- or marketing-driven culture.

## Market Position
- **Production Volume:** Frequently surpasses [[Tesla]] in quarterly global pure-electric vehicle production.
- **Valuation Paradox:** As discussed in the Zhihu analysis, its market capitalization has historically been lower than Tesla's, illustrating the tension between **Financial vs. Industrial Logic**.
- **Global Expansion:** Actively expanding sales beyond China into Europe, Southeast Asia, and other regions.

## Financial Backing
Notably, [[Warren Buffett]]'s Berkshire Hathaway invested in BYD in 2008. His subsequent reduction of holdings is interpreted in the source discussion as a normal portfolio cycle, not a critique of BYD's fundamentals.
