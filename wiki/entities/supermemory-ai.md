# Supermemory.ai

## Overview
Supermemory.ai is a startup company that developed the experimental ASMR (Agentic Search and Memory Retrieval) system. Based on the critical analysis presented in [[Supermemory's ASMR System: A Critical Look at AI Memory Breakthroughs]], the company positioned itself with a focus on practical engineering and commercial viability, despite creating a high-scoring research prototype that was intentionally not designed for direct productization.

## Key Action: The ASMR "Social Experiment"
The company's most notable public action was the development and publication of results for the **ASMR system**, which achieved approximately 99% accuracy on the rigorous [[LongMemEval/LongMemEval_s]] benchmark. Crucially, they framed this achievement not merely as a technical breakthrough but as a **"social experiment"** intended to provoke discussion within the AI community.

## Stated Philosophy & Critique
Through this experiment, Supermemory.ai aimed to highlight:
1.  **The Limits of Vector Databases:** Arguing that traditional vector similarity search "hits a dead end" for complex memory tasks involving time, logic, and contradiction.
2.  **The Benchmarking Gap:** Demonstrating the vast chasm between achieving State-of-the-Art (SOTA) academic scores and building a commercially viable, low-latency, cost-effective production system—a concept described as the [[Benchmark vs. Engineering Reality Gap]].
3.  **Advocacy for New Paradigms:** Championing a shift towards [[Agent-Based Active Retrieval]] and [[Multi-Agent Specialization & Orchestration]] as the future of AI memory and complex reasoning tasks.

## Relation to ASMR
Supermemory.ai is the entity that conceived and built the [[ASMR (Agentic Search and Memory Retrieval)]] architecture. The company's identity is thus tightly coupled with the technical and philosophical arguments embodied by that system.
