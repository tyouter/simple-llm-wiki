# 郑坚义

## Description
A product manager and recognized "excellent respondent" (优秀答主) on Zhihu. In the [[Zhihu Discussion: The Role of 'Scenarios' in Product Management]], he provided crucial methodological context by framing [[Scenario Analysis (场景分析)]] within the established discipline of [[User-Centered Design (UCD)]].

## Key Contribution
He connected the discussion about scenarios to a broader, standardized product design toolkit, helping to ground the practice in existing professional methodology. He explicitly positioned scenario analysis as one of several interrelated tools, rather than a standalone technique.

## UCD Toolkit Integration
He outlined the relationship between three core UCD tools for requirement analysis:
1.  **[[Persona (用户画像)]]**: A representation of a user segment, answering "*Who* are we designing for?"
2.  **Scenario (场景)**: The narrative context, answering "*When, where, and why* would they use it?" and "*What* is their story?"
3.  **[[Use Case (用例)]]**: The specific interaction sequence, answering "*How* do they use it to accomplish a task?"

His perspective clarifies that scenarios are the "connective tissue" between understanding the user (Persona) and specifying system behavior (Use Case). A scenario gives meaning and context to both the persona's attributes and the steps in a use case.

## Impact on the Discussion
By providing this framework, he elevated the conversation from anecdotal tips to a principled approach to product design. It helped other readers see scenario analysis not as a new trend, but as a core component of a mature, user-centered design process.
