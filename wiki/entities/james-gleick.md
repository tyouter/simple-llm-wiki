# James Gleick

## Overview
James Gleick is an American author, journalist, and biographer known for his writings on science and technology, particularly the history and cultural impact of information theory and chaos theory.

## Relevance to Knowledge Management
He is quoted at the conclusion of the [[Zhihu Discussion: Comprehensive Guide to High-Quality Information Sources for Learning and Career Development]] with the seminal line:

> **"When information is cheap, attention becomes expensive."**

This quote, likely from his work *The Information: A History, A Theory, A Flood*, perfectly encapsulates the core problem the Zhihu guide addresses. In an age of information abundance, the scarce resource is no longer access to information, but the *curated attention* required to find high-fidelity, valuable information.

## Philosophical Connection
Gleick's work explores how the nature of information itself shapes society, science, and thought. Citing him elevates the discussion from practical tips to a philosophical principle: building a [[Self-Directed Learning Infrastructure]] and practicing [[Information Source Curation]] are essential defenses against the cognitive costs of information overload. They are methods for strategically allocating one's "expensive" attention.

His inclusion signifies that the guide's author views effective information management not just as a productivity hack, but as a critical response to a fundamental shift in the human condition brought about by the information age.
