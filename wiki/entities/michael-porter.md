# Michael Porter

## Description
A foundational management theorist and Harvard Business School professor known for his work on competitive strategy and national competitive advantage. Porter's frameworks are considered essential reading in strategic management education.

## Key Contributions

### The Porter Trilogy
Three foundational works frequently referenced in strategic management:
1. **Competitive Strategy** (1980): Introduces the Five Forces framework for industry analysis
2. **Competitive Advantage** (1985): Develops the value chain concept and generic strategies
3. **National Competitive Advantage** (1990): Presents the Diamond Model for national competitiveness

## Role in the Discussion
In the Zhihu discussion about career transition to strategic management, Porter's works are included in [[Sunny]]'s 'minimum required reading list' for entering the field.
