# Robert S. Kaplan & David P. Norton

## Description
Management theorists known for developing the Balanced Scorecard and Strategy Map frameworks. Their work focuses on translating strategy into measurable objectives and aligning organizational activities with strategic goals.

## Key Contributions

### Balanced Scorecard
A strategic planning and management system that goes beyond traditional financial measures to include:
1. **Financial Perspective**
2. **Customer Perspective**
3. **Internal Business Processes**
4. **Learning and Growth Perspective**

### Strategy Map
A visual representation of an organization's strategy that shows the cause-and-effect relationships between strategic objectives across the four Balanced Scorecard perspectives.

## Role in the Discussion
In the Zhihu career transition discussion, Kaplan and Norton's works are included in [[Sunny]]'s recommended reading list for aspiring strategic consultants. Their frameworks represent important tools for [[Theory-Practice Integration]]—bridging high-level strategy with operational implementation.

## Importance for Strategic Management
- Provides practical tools for implementing and measuring strategy
- Bridges the gap between strategic planning and operational execution
- Represents the type of framework knowledge needed in modern consulting engagements

## Connection to Learning Frameworks
These frameworks would typically be studied after foundational theory (like [[Michael Porter]]'s work) in a [[Strategic Management Learning Framework]], as they provide tools for application and measurement rather than pure strategy formulation.

# Robert S. Kaplan & David P. Norton

## Identity
Management theorists known for developing the Balanced Scorecard and Strategy Map frameworks.

## Key Contributions

### Balanced Scorecard (1992)
- A strategic planning and management system
- Translates vision and strategy into objectives and measures across four perspectives:
  1. **Financial**: Traditional measures of success
  2. **Customer**: Value proposition and satisfaction
  3. **Internal Processes**: Operational excellence
  4. **Learning & Growth**: Organizational capabilities and innovation
- Balances financial and non-financial measures, short-term and long-term objectives

### Strategy Map (2000)
- Visual representation of strategy showing cause-and-effect relationships
- Links objectives across the four Balanced Scorecard perspectives
- Communicates strategy clearly throughout the organization
- Shows how intangible assets (like skills and culture) create tangible results

## Source Context
In the [[Zhihu Discussion: Career Transition to Strategic Management and Consulting]], Kaplan and Norton's works are included in the application layer of [[Sunny]]'s [[Strategic Management Learning Framework]]. They represent practical frameworks for implementing and measuring strategy.

## Philosophical Approach
- **Measurement Focus**: Belief that "what gets measured gets managed"
- **Strategy Communication**: Emphasis on making strategy understandable and actionable
- **Causal Relationships**: Focus on how different strategic elements connect and influence each other
- **Implementation Orientation**: Practical tools for executing strategy, not just formulating it

## Relation to Other Strategic Thinking
While [[Michael E. Porter]]'s work focuses on competitive positioning and industry analysis, Kaplan and Norton address how to implement and measure strategy execution. Their frameworks help build the [[Theory-to-Practice Bridge in Strategy]] by providing concrete tools for application.

## Lasting Impact
- **Widespread Adoption**: Used by thousands of organizations worldwide
- **Evolution**: Framework has expanded to include strategy-focused organization concepts
- **Integration**: Often used alongside other strategic frameworks rather than as a replacement
