# Li Shufu (李书福)

## Overview
Li Shufu is the founder and Chairman of [[Geely (吉利汽车)]], one of China's largest and most internationally active automotive manufacturers. Starting from humble beginnings (making refrigerator parts, then motorcycles), he built Geely into a global player. In the [[Zhihu Discussion: Financial vs. Industrial Logic in BYD's Development Strategy]], he is mentioned as an example of a hardworking, successful industrialist whose company nonetheless experienced severe stock market depreciation.

## Entrepreneurial Journey
Known for his tenacity and "can-do" spirit, Li famously said, "Cars are just four wheels and a sofa," reflecting his pragmatic, de-mystifying approach to a complex industry. He led Geely's landmark acquisition of Volvo Cars from Ford in 2010, a move that initially drew skepticism but is now widely seen as a success.

## Role in the Analysis
The Zhihu discussion uses Li Shufu and Geely's stock performance to illustrate a key point: individual entrepreneurial effort and industrial success do not guarantee favorable treatment from financial markets. This underscores the concept of the **[[Elevator Effect/电梯效应]]**, where sector-wide trends can dominate a stock's fate.

## Implicit Contrast with Wang Chuanfu
While both Li Shufu and [[Wang Chuanfu (王传福)]] of [[BYD (比亚迪)]] are celebrated Chinese industrialists, their strategic paths differ:
- **Li Shufu/Geely:** Growth through bold international acquisitions and multi-brand management.
- **Wang Chuanfu/BYD:** Growth through deep [[Vertical Integration/闭环]] and in-house technological innovation.
The discussion suggests that in the specific period analyzed, the market (applying **Financial Logic**) punished Geely's strategy while being relatively cooler on BYD's industrial achievements, highlighting the tension between the two logics.
