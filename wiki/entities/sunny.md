## Contribution to Discussion
In the [[Zhihu Discussion: Career Transition to Strategic Management and Consulting]], Sunny provided a detailed "minimum required reading list" for learning strategy, representing a comprehensive [[Strategic Management Learning Framework]].

## Learning Framework Structure
Sunny's approach progresses through three layers:

### Layer 1: Foundation
- Macro analysis understanding
- Core strategy texts including the "Porter Trilogy" by [[Michael E. Porter]]
- Basic strategic concepts and frameworks

### Layer 2: Expansion
- Functional knowledge in key business areas:
  - R&D and innovation
  - Marketing and sales
  - Finance and accounting
  - Operations and supply chain
  - Human resources and organization

### Layer 3: Application
- Practical frameworks for implementation
- Performance measurement systems
- Strategic planning processes
- Includes works by [[Robert S. Kaplan & David P. Norton]] on the [[Balanced Scorecard]] and [[Strategy Map]]

## Perspective on Strategic Learning
- **Systematic Approach**: Emphasizes structured, progressive learning
- **Comprehensive Coverage**: Believes in building broad knowledge before specialization
- **Time Investment**: Acknowledges that proper strategic education requires significant study (potentially 1-2 years of dedicated reading)
- **Foundation-First**: Stresses the importance of theoretical foundations before practical application

## Experience Basis
With nearly 20 years in strategic management, Sunny's recommendations likely reflect:
- Personal learning journey and what proved valuable
- Observation of what knowledge gaps hinder strategic effectiveness
- Understanding of what foundational knowledge enables more advanced strategic thinking
