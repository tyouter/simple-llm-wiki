# 刘飞

## Description
A product manager and recognized "excellent respondent" (优秀答主) on Zhihu. In the [[Zhihu Discussion: The Role of 'Scenarios' in Product Management]], he provided one of the most systematic and structured frameworks for conducting [[Scenario Analysis (场景分析)]].

## Key Contribution
He moved the discussion from philosophical distinction to practical methodology, offering a clear template for deconstructing and describing a scenario. His framework suggests analyzing a scenario by breaking it down into specific components to ensure comprehensiveness.

## Systematic Framework for Scenario Analysis
According to his contribution, a robust scenario description should include:
1.  **User Identity & Role**: Who is the user in this context? (e.g., a commuting professional, a first-time parent).
2.  **Motivation & Goal**: What are they fundamentally trying to achieve or obtain? (e.g., relax during commute, ensure baby's safety).
3.  **Context & Environment**: The physical/digital setting and timing (the traditional "where/when").
4.  **Process & Challenges**: The sequence of actions they take and the specific obstacles or pain points they encounter.
5.  **Emotional Needs & State**: Their feelings during the process (frustration, anxiety, hope) and the emotional outcome they desire (relief, confidence, joy).
6.  **Product's Role**: A clear statement of how the product intervenes in this story to help overcome challenges and achieve the goal.

## Illustrative Examples
He applied this framework to diverse products:
- **Smartphone Camera**: Analyzing the scenario of "capturing a child's first steps" versus "scanning a document" leads to vastly different feature priorities (burst mode & auto-focus vs. perspective correction & edge detection).
- **E-commerce & On-Demand Services**: Demonstrated how scenario analysis clarifies market positioning by defining the specific "job" a user hires a product for in a given moment.

His answer provides a actionable guide for product teams seeking to implement scenario-driven thinking.
