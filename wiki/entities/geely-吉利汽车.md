# Geely (吉利汽车)

## Overview
Geely Automobile Holdings Ltd. is a major Chinese automotive manufacturer, headquartered in Hangzhou. Founded by [[Li Shufu (李书福)]], it has grown through aggressive expansion and international acquisitions, most notably Volvo Cars (2010), a stake in Daimler AG (2018), and Lotus. In the [[Zhihu Discussion: Financial vs. Industrial Logic in BYD's Development Strategy]], Geely is used as a contrasting case to [[BYD (比亚迪)]].

## Strategic Profile
1. **Growth via Acquisition:** Unlike BYD's organic, vertically-integrated growth, Geely expanded its technology portfolio and global footprint primarily through purchasing established foreign brands (Volvo, Lotus) and forming alliances (with Daimler).
2. **Brand Portfolio Strategy:** Manages multiple brands (Geely, Lynk & Co, Zeekr, Volvo, Polestar, Lotus) targeting different market segments.
3. **Technology Sharing:** Aims to leverage technology (especially from Volvo) across its brand portfolio.

## Role in the Zhihu Analysis
The discussion cites Geely's significant stock price decline over a period as a key example. Despite the hard work of its founder Li Shufu and clear strategic efforts, the company's market valuation suffered. This is attributed to the **[[Elevator Effect/电梯效应]]**, where broader market sentiment towards the automotive sector or Chinese stocks outweighed company-specific efforts.

## Contrast with BYD
- **Strategy:** Geely's acquisition-led approach vs. BYD's [[Vertical Integration/闭环]] and in-house technology development.
- **Market Perception:** Both are major Chinese automakers, but the discussion suggests the financial markets rewarded their strategies differently during the period analyzed, highlighting the unpredictability and sometimes disconnect of **Financial Logic**.
- **Leadership:** Both founders (Li Shufu and [[Wang Chuanfu (王传福)]]) are portrayed as dedicated industrialists, yet their companies' stock performance diverged.
