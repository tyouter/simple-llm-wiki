# Ethan Mollick

## Description
A professor at the Wharton School who studies innovation, entrepreneurship, and the impact of artificial intelligence on work and education. Provided a measured assessment of [[Google Stitch]] following its repositioning as a 'vibe design platform.'

## Assessment of Google Stitch
Mollick noted that Stitch applies 'vibework' beyond coding to design, with impressive results that feel more natural for non-programmers. His analysis highlights:

1. **Concept Extension:** Recognition that [[Vibe Coding]] principles are being extended to design through [[Vibe Design]]
2. **Accessibility:** AI design tools may lower barriers for non-designers
3. **Natural Interaction:** Text-to-interface feels more intuitive than traditional design tools for some users
4. **Measured Perspective:** Acknowledges both potential and limitations of AI design tools

## Broader Context
Mollick's work often examines how AI transforms professional work and skills. His assessment of Stitch fits within this research focus, considering:
- How AI tools change design workflows and required skills
- The balance between automation and human creativity
- The accessibility implications of natural language interfaces

## Significance in AI Design Discourse
As an academic researcher rather than a tool vendor or direct competitor, Mollick provides a relatively neutral perspective on the emergence of AI design tools like Stitch. His assessment adds credibility to discussions about [[AI-Induced Industry Disruption]] and the evolution of [[AI-Native Design Workflow]].
