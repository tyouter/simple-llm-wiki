# David Autor

## Context in Discussion
The work of this MIT economist is cited by [[Token侦探]] in the Zhihu discussion [[Zhihu Discussion: The Era of Super Individuals and Agent Shock]] to provide a historical analogy and academic foundation for the predicted **"Agent Shock."**

## Key Research: The "China Shock"
Autor is renowned for his empirical research on the impact of Chinese import competition on the U.S. labor market in the 1990s and 2000s. His work documented:
- Significant job displacement in U.S. manufacturing.
- Prolonged wage depression and worker adjustment difficulties in affected regions.
- The profound and localized economic disruption caused by the sudden globalization of a major industrial sector.

## Analogy to "Agent Shock"
Token侦探 draws a direct parallel:
- **China Shock**: Globalization of *manufacturing* trade → displacement of blue-collar workers.
- **Agent Shock** (predicted): Globalization of *service* trade via AI Agents → potential displacement and pressure on white-collar professionals.

The citation lends economic credibility and a sense of historical precedent to the forecast of large-scale disruption from the AI-enabled globalization of services.
