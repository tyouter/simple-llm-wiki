# 宙巡

## Identity
A contributor on the Chinese Q&A platform Zhihu, identified as a parent and an educator who holds a teaching certificate. He writes from the perspective of both observing his daughter's education and applying pedagogical methods.

## Contribution
Author of the primary and most detailed answer in the [[Zhihu Discussion: Learning Method Transition Crisis]]. His analysis forms the core thesis of the discussion, focusing on the cognitive and methodological roots of the high school transition crisis.

## Core Argument
He attributes the academic decline of previously successful students (often girls) to a catastrophic failure of learning strategy. He argues that junior high rewards [[Enumeration Path Dependency]]—exhaustive memorization and pattern-matching—which completely breaks down in the face of high school's abstract and complex material. This creates a "知识结构坍塌" (collapse of the knowledge structure).

## Proposed Solution
He advocates for a fundamental shift to [[Derivation-First Learning]], a philosophy of building knowledge upward from foundational principles ("道生一"). He provides concrete examples of teaching his daughter using this method, emphasizing understanding over memorization. He also introduces the concept of [[Knowledge Compression]] as the desired outcome of this process.

## Teaching Philosophy
- **Anti-Memorization**: Strongly opposes early rote memorization (e.g., of multiplication tables) as it creates harmful dependencies and wastes mental "memory space."
- **Principle-Centered**: Believes all knowledge should be traceable to core definitions.
- **Systematic Building**: Views education as constructing a robust, efficient "knowledge graph" in the student's mind, akin to good software or system design (relatable to [[Domain-Specific Language (DSL) for AI Agents]]).
