# Michael E. Porter

## Identity
Renowned strategy professor at Harvard Business School and author of foundational works on competitive strategy.

## Foundational Contributions
Porter is best known for the "Porter Trilogy," which forms the theoretical foundation for much of modern strategic management:

### 1. Competitive Strategy (1980)
- Introduces the **Five Forces Framework** for industry analysis
- Defines three generic strategies: Cost Leadership, Differentiation, and Focus
- Establishes systematic approach to strategic positioning

### 2. Competitive Advantage (1985)
- Develops the **Value Chain** concept for analyzing firm activities
- Explains how competitive advantage is created and sustained
- Introduces cost drivers and differentiation drivers

### 3. National Competitive Advantage (1990)
- Presents the **Diamond Model** for national competitive advantage
- Explains why certain nations succeed in particular industries
- Links firm strategy to national economic policy

## Lasting Influence
- **Framework Durability**: Porter's models remain widely used decades after publication
- **Academic Foundation**: His work established strategy as a distinct management discipline
- **Practical Application**: His frameworks are taught in business schools worldwide and used by consultants and executives

## Relation to Other Concepts
Porter's work represents the classical foundation of strategic thinking that later approaches (like the [[Balanced Scorecard]] by [[Robert S. Kaplan & David P. Norton]]) build upon or complement.
