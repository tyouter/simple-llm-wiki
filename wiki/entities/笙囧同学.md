# 笙囧同学 (Sheng Jiong Tongxue)

## Identity
Zhihu user who contributed a reflective, experience-based answer to the [[Zhihu Discussion: Optimizing Claude Code with CLAUDE.md and Efficient Development Workflows]].

## Contribution and Perspective
笙囧同学 shared practical insights gained from two months of hands-on trial and error with configuring [[CLAUDE.md]] for [[Claude Code]]. Their perspective represents a **pragmatic, empirical** middle ground.

### Key Findings and Conclusions:
1.  **The Brevity Discovery**: Initially created a very long CLAUDE.md file (approximately 2000 words). Through use, they observed that the AI's performance and adherence to instructions **decreased** with the overly long file.
2.  **Optimal Length**: Through iteration, they found the "sweet spot" to be a much shorter file of about 800 words (~1/3 the original length).
3.  **Emphasis on Clarity**: Concluded that the effectiveness of CLAUDE.md depends more on the **clarity and salience** of its rules than on their quantity. Critical, well-phrased instructions outperform exhaustive lists.
4.  **Practical Refinement**: Their approach embodies an empirical, test-and-refine methodology for prompt engineering.

## Philosophical Stance
Their stance is pragmatic and user-experience driven. It supports the principle that "less is more" in AI instruction, not from a theoretical standpoint like [[yahah]], but from observed results. It validates the minimalist argument with empirical data.

## Relation to Other Contributors
- **Validates [[yahah]]'s Minimalism**: Provides real-world evidence that supports yahah's argument for concise CLAUDE.md files, though arriving at it independently through experimentation.
- **Contrasts with [[白玉京]]'s Comprehensiveness**: Their experience of reduced effectiveness with a long file directly challenges the utility of the highly detailed approach advocated by 白玉京.
