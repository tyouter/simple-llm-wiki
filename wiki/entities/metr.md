# METR

## Description
Research organization that measures AI model capabilities.

## Key Research Findings
### AI Model Capability Measurement
METR's research provides empirical data on the exponential improvement of AI models, which underpins the [[AI-Exponential Product Management]] paradigm.

### Specific Measurement
According to METR's research referenced by [[Cat Wu]]:
- **Claude Opus 4.6** can complete tasks that take humans nearly 12 hours in about half of cases

## Importance for Product Management
### Quantifying Exponential Improvement
METR's measurements provide concrete evidence for:
- The pace of AI capability improvement
- The practical impact of new model versions
- The rationale for changing product management approaches

### Informing Development Decisions
Product teams can use METR's research to:
- Understand what new capabilities each model version provides
- Make data-driven decisions about when to update features
- Justify investments in [[Model-Capability-Driven Product Evolution]]

## Relation to Organizational Change
The empirical evidence from METR supports the organizational shifts described by Cat Wu at [[Anthropic]], including:
- Moving to [[Prototype-First Development]] (because capabilities change too fast for long planning cycles)
- Adopting the [[Simplest Working Solution Principle]] (because today's limitations may be gone tomorrow)
- Implementing [[Edge Exploration Projects (Side Quests)]] (to discover new capabilities as they emerge)

## Broader Context
Organizations like METR play a crucial role in the AI ecosystem by:
- Providing objective measurements of model capabilities
- Enabling comparison across different AI systems
- Documenting the pace of technological progress
- Informing both technical and business decisions
